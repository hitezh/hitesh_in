Did you know that AI is now being used to enhance winemaking and reduce ferry emissions? This week's AI news highlights the rapid evolution and far-reaching impact of this transformative technology. From China's Manus AI sparking debate to India's bold investments in its AI ecosystem, let's explore the key developments that are shaping the future of AI.
**Global AI Highlights**
- **Manus Mania:** China's Manus AI is generating significant buzz with its advanced digital assistant capabilities. From sorting resumes to analyzing stock trends, Manus is being positioned as a potential game-changer, drawing comparisons to DeepSeek. However Manus is already facing skepticism, with some questioning the capabilities and implications of these so-called "PhD-level" AI agents. Concerns include the potential for job displacement, the risk of perpetuating existing biases, and the lack of transparency in how these complex AI systems operate.
- **AI in Unexpected Places:** AI is no longer confined to the tech world. This week's news highlights its growing influence across diverse industries. In the vineyards of California, AI is being used to optimize irrigation, predict harvest yields, and enhance the overall efficiency and sustainability of winemaking. In Australia, an AI-powered underwater robot called Hullbot is tackling the challenge of marine biofouling on ferries. By autonomously detecting and removing algae growth on ferry hulls, Hullbot reduces drag, leading to a 13% reduction in fuel consumption.
- **ServiceNow Acquires Moveworks:** In a significant acquisition, ServiceNow has acquired Moveworks, an AI-powered enterprise assistant company, for $2.85 billion. Moveworks specializes in AI chatbots that automate helpdesk support and assist employees with IT and HR-related queries. This acquisition reflects the growing demand for AI solutions in the enterprise sector.
- **Apple Delays Siri AI Features (again):** Apple has reportedly delayed the rollout of its next-generation AI-powered Siri, which was expected to bring major improvements to the iPhone's digital assistant. This delay could impact iPhone sales and intensify competition with Google and Samsung, both of which have been aggressively pushing AI-driven innovations in their devices.
- **Palantir's AIPCon:** Palantir recently hosted its AIPCon event, where it showcased its latest AI solutions and highlighted its growing customer base. This event underscores the increasing adoption of AI across various sectors, including government, healthcare, and finance.

**Indian AI Landscape**
- **Investment Fuels Innovation:** Change Engine's $5 million investment in early-stage Indian AI startups over the next five years is a significant boost to India's AI ecosystem. This funding will support promising AI ventures and accelerate the development of innovative solutions across various sectors.
- **Jobs and Skills:** A Bain & Company report predicts that India's AI sector will create 2.3 million jobs by 2027. However, to fully capitalize on this opportunity, India needs to address the potential AI talent crunch by reskilling over 1 million workers. Initiatives like upskilling programs and educational reforms will be crucial to bridge the skills gap and ensure that India has the workforce to support its AI ambitions.
- **Maharashtra's AI Focus:** The Maharashtra Budget has allocated Rs 9,710 crore for the agriculture department, with a focus on leveraging AI to improve farming practices and increase productivity. This investment highlights the growing recognition of AI's potential to address challenges in key sectors like agriculture.
- **Bollywood Embraces AI:** Actor Ajay Devgn has launched a new AI company called Prismix. This move reflects the increasing interest in AI across various sectors in India, including the entertainment industry.
